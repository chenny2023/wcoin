## What is Tekel Data and what does it actually do?

Tekel Data is a transparent, on-chain data layer for the iGaming industry. It maps the public wallets of crypto casino operators across 11+ blockchains, reads their real deposit and withdrawal flows, and publishes proof of reserves and aggregated trust scores. The platform is free to use and requires no login.

The core problem it addresses is industry opacity. Operators self-report their numbers, "volume" is often inflated by wash trading, and most reviews are paid placements. Tekel Data is the opposite by design — a radically transparent, public data layer where nothing is taken on trust.

## How does Tekel Data calculate real volume instead of inflated numbers?

Operators can inflate their numbers by routing funds between internal hot wallets or counting market-maker flows as real player deposits. Tekel Data tracks 11+ public blockchains and cleans the raw data by removing internal hot wallet transfers, double-counting, and market-maker fund flows. What remains is the net external deposit and withdrawal volume — the actual money moving in and out from real players.

This matters because a casino can claim massive volume on its homepage, but if 80% of that volume is just the same funds bouncing between two wallets the operator controls, the number is meaningless. You can see the cleaned, real-volume figures on the [highest volume crypto casinos](https://www.tekeldata.com/highest-volume-crypto-casinos) page.

## How does the proof of reserves feature work?

Tekel Data maps and monitors the on-chain wallets of 47 operators and reads their multi-chain reserves in real time. The total tracked reserves are approximately $289.5 million. The platform displays each operator's reserves and coverage ratio, updated roughly every 30 minutes.

This is not a self-attested proof of reserves where the operator hands over a signed message. It is an independent, third-party mapping of publicly visible on-chain wallets. If an operator's reserves drop sharply — say, a significant 7-day decline — that event gets logged. You can check current reserve status on the [proof of reserves](https://www.tekeldata.com/proof-of-reserves) page.

## Are the trust scores just another paid ranking?

No. Tekel Data does not accept affiliate marketing payments for rankings. The blended trust score aggregates public ratings from independent third parties including Casino.guru, AskGamblers, Casino.org, and Trustpilot.

A platform only qualifies for the ranking if it has at least 2 verified independent sources. The scores are then weighted into a composite rating. This means an operator cannot simply buy a good review from one site and appear at the top — it needs consistent, independent corroboration. The full methodology and current standings are on the [trust rankings](https://www.tekeldata.com/rankings/trust) page.

## What happens if a casino's reserves drop or something looks wrong?

Tekel Data maintains a neutral risk registry that monitors and discloses on-chain reserve events. If an operator's reserves drop sharply over a 7-day window, or if the coverage ratio becomes abnormal, that event is recorded and surfaced.

This is designed to give users an early warning signal before they deposit. Rather than finding out a platform is insolvent after a withdrawal fails, you can check the [risk registry](https://www.tekeldata.com/risk) to see if there have been recent reserve anomalies. For a deeper look at how individual operators are profiled, the single operator on-chain profiles page breaks down wallet activity per platform.

No account is needed. The platform is free and accessible without login. You can look up operator reserves, real volume, trust scores, and risk events directly.
