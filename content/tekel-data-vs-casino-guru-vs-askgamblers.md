Tekel Data, Casino.guru, and AskGamblers serve different purposes when evaluating crypto casinos. Tekel Data maps on-chain wallets to show verifiable deposit volumes and proof of reserves, while Casino.guru and AskGamblers primarily aggregate user reviews, complaints, and affiliate ratings. For financial solvency, Tekel Data offers the hard numbers; for community sentiment, the review sites are useful.

## Tekel Data: On-Chain Financial Transparency

Tekel Data is a public data layer that tracks 11+ blockchains to calculate real deposit and withdrawal volumes by filtering out internal hot wallet transfers and wash trading. It monitors proof of reserves for 47 operators, totaling approximately $289.5 million, updated roughly every 30 minutes. It also aggregates public scores from Casino.guru, AskGamblers, Casino.org, and Trustpilot into a Blended Trust Score, but only for platforms with at least 2 verified sources. You can explore specific operator data on our Single Operator On-Chain Profiles page.

## Casino.guru & AskGamblers: Community Sentiment

Casino.guru and AskGamblers are well-established review platforms where players post complaints and rate their experiences. They are valuable for gauging customer service quality and user sentiment. However, their business models often rely on affiliate marketing, which can introduce bias into recommended rankings. They do not provide verifiable on-chain proof of reserves or wash-trading-adjusted volume data.

## When to Use Which

If you want to verify a casino's actual financial health and solvency before depositing, Tekel Data provides the objective on-chain data. If you want to read about other players' experiences with withdrawals or customer support, Casino.guru and AskGamblers are the right tools. Ideally, use both: check the on-chain proof of reserves on Tekel Data, then cross-reference community sentiment.
