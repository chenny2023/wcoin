## Why is your crypto gambling withdrawal delayed or stuck?

Your crypto gambling withdrawal is likely delayed because the casino's hot wallet is temporarily depleted, or worse, the platform lacks the underlying funds to pay you. While legitimate platforms process payouts quickly, repeated delays often signal a solvency issue. To protect your funds, you need to verify the casino's actual on-chain liquidity before you ever make a deposit. 

When you request a withdrawal, the casino must move funds from its operational hot wallet to your address. If that wallet is empty or flagged, the transaction stalls. To understand the broader policies around payout holds and identity checks, you can read our guide on [Crypto Casino Withdrawals & KYC Policies](/guide/withdrawals-kyc).

## How to check if a crypto casino can actually pay you

You can troubleshoot a delayed withdrawal—or prevent one entirely—by checking the blockchain yourself. Here is how to verify a casino's payout capacity:

1. **Locate their public wallet addresses:** Find the casino's deposit and hot wallet addresses. 
2. **Check hot wallet balances:** Look at the current balance of their active payout wallet. If it sits at near zero for days, they cannot process withdrawals without moving funds from cold storage or new deposits.
3. **Trace incoming deposits:** Are they paying existing players using the deposits from new players? This is a massive red flag.
4. **Review their Proof of Reserves (PoR):** A legitimate platform holds enough reserves to cover all user balances.

## What does a sudden reserve drop mean for your withdrawal?

If a casino experiences a sudden, unexplained drop in its on-chain reserves, your withdrawal is at immediate risk. A healthy platform maintains stable or growing reserves proportional to its player base. If reserves drop by 30% or more within a 7-day window without a clear public explanation, it often indicates internal financial stress, a hack, or an impending exit scam.

## How to spot fake volume that hides insolvency

Some casinos advertise massive daily betting volumes to appear financially healthy, but these numbers are often inflated by wash trading. They cycle funds between their own internal hot wallets and treasury accounts to create the illusion of high player activity. When you try to withdraw, you realize they have no real liquidity.

To avoid this trap, look at verified transaction volumes rather than advertised numbers. By stripping out internal hot wallet churn, double-counting, and treasury movements, you can see the actual deposit and withdrawal flow.
